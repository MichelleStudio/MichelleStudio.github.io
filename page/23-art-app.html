Reduction, 2023
3D rendered still of a video
1920px x 1080px
Full Scale
Pruning is paramount in real-world gardening. To be able to prune, to cut off redundancies, is to be able to "train" and invest in a living organism. However, in the digital world there is no clean removal, no real reduction. 'Reduction' questions the obsoleteness of once useful tools in a space that is forever multiplying and expanding.

3D rendered still of a video
1440px x 810px
Full Scale
Snakes and cherry blossoms both are symbols of Spring, a time of renewal and the potential for change. Remixed into the digital landscape, "time" passes but nothing will ever change. In an infinite cycle of day and night, 'Transient" compares the ephemeral and preserved, and interrogates our faith in digitalization.

Ara Pacis South Procession Restoration, 2023
Digital color restoration
19616px x 3448px
In antiquity, almost every statue and frieze of importance was painted vibrantly as if it really was alive. However, these days, only traces of the pigments remain. Today, the Ara Pacis--a celebration of peace--remains somberly gray. Through guided research, I restored and recolored the South Procession, bringing the parade back to its original liveliness non-destructively.
Natural Progression, 2023
Generative Art (JPGs, MP3s, Javascript)
1396px x 783px
Live Link
"Natural Progression" is a digital simulation of high-risk California native species population growth and decay. A proposal for a full-room installation, the population of digital plants rely solely on human disturbance (walking, talking, sudden noises). It is an interactive contemplation of the human relation to nature and if coexistence is possible.

SF Chinatown: Mapped, 2023
Web Art (SVG, text, Javascript)
1440px x 810px
Live Link
San Francisco's Chinatown remains both a racial confine and a place for cultural community. It is in one way an adaptation to new society and a replica of China. In a modern interpretation of this historic space, visitors are encouraged to interact with the physical space of SF Chinatown, while uncovering the Confucius tenets that underpin its development and values.
Deliberate, 2023
set of 4, digital prints on vellum.
5' w x 8' h x 6" d
This set of illustrations draws inspiration from Li Bai's "Drinking Alone under the Moon," an exploration of existence through metaphors of drunkenness. Beginning alone under a flowering tree at full moon, Li Bai ascends from reality to the fantastical–contemplating ideas of ambitions and life intertwined with luck and nature. In the end, he sinks back down to reality–sitting alone under the moon once again. Hung in float frames in a wave-like pattern, Deliberate gently sways due to undetectable currents and invites the viewer to contemplate one's own ambitions in the complex nature of society.

Occupation, 2022
wool/nylon blend and polyester, horn toggle buttons.
54"w x 48"h x 2"d
Occupation is a European-like coat constructed using endemic Chinese sewing techniques—proposing an alternate history where authentic form is preserved through co-opted material use. The base of this two piece set is the qipao, the traditional dress of the 1800s, a time where China--specifically Shanghai--was forcefully occupied by western countries. Pressured to conform to western ideals, Chinese sewing techniques have all but been lost. Inspired by these "international settlements," Occupation has been made out of materials from the imperial culture, (Wool instead of silk, a coat instead of a dress, horn toggles instead of frog knots).
