MAKING OF: Essence

Is it North, West, South, East or East, South, West, North? Either way, relative positioning has always been important to society, and ancient Chinese culture was no exception. East, South, West, North became Blue, Red, Black, White—Dragon, Phoenix, Tiger, Tortoise. The seasons, the virtues, the elements and guardians of the central Yellow Emperor—these Four Auspicious Beasts are representative of the entirety of Chinese heritage.

However, the world cannot be simplified this way. There is no central “Us” requiring protection from all directions. Remixing into the contemporary, Essence is based on the bones of the Beasts and the new life that can grow from it.

Skulls, Flowers, Patterns, and Colors

None of the Auspicious Beasts actually exist. To ground the symbols in reality, I chose similar, somewhat recognizable animals and referenced their skulls. Dragon became the lizard komodo dragon, Phoenix as a crane, tortoise as a turtle, and tiger as a sabertooth skull--a diaspora of creatures to represent the diaspora of humans.

Flowers were chosen by their derivative common names and overall form: dragonfruit flower, bird of paradise, la mei (shell-like petals), and tiger lily. It is the life that continues, wherever in the world.

Background patterns were chosen to match the elemental and virtue aspects of the Beasts. Geometric waves as the endless water for Dragon, panchi (hornless dragons) for the dragon-equal Phoenix, lotus wreaths for the steadfast Tortoise, and the never ending lattice for the vigorous Tiger.

Each illustration is predominately the color of its Beast: Blue, Red, Black, and White sourced from the Tang dynasty colors. Yellow circles are placed behind each skull, uniting each direction as a new center and the dawn of its own form in a new world.
