MAKING OF: Cranes

I don't quite remember when I learned how to fold an origami crane, but I do know that it is the only one I can ever remember. It is both the most basic beginner model and the most classic, well-respected design. It's been a habit of mine to fold cranes to pass the time—turning discarded scraps into fun little birds which, in turn, get discarded once again into the trash bin when class ends.

But discards and cranes, short-lived waste and the symbol of longevity—something is going on here. I've accumulated 200 of them and yet I'm still not quite sure what it is yet. Mostly made out of the shiny Ferroro Rocher wrappers, there's some receipts mixed in there as well.

I've decided I need them to move as a swarm, to not be static—to be a little hypnotic and unassuming. However, that requires motors and electric wirings...things I did like once in middle school.

So far, I have wanted to have a wave-like motion, built out of laser cut gears powered by a stepper motor. Initial tests have run into problems: friction, how to place attachment (from the top or the bottom), etc.... It might need to be simplified.
