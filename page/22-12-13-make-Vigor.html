MAKING OF: VIGOR

California is home to many biomes--from ocean and lakes to desert and snow, and even high mountains as well as low-lying marshland. In a way, it has a little bit of everything. In particular, the desert--specifically Joshua Tree National Park--is more than it seems.

Not just Joshua Trees

Though taken at Joshua Tree National Park, Vigor does not depict one of the numerous--though endangered--Joshua trees. There are numerous other species, life, and history attached to the region. This photograph was taken at the Hidden Valley, a gentle depression surrounded by a few tall boulder formations. These slight geographical coincidences creates a slightly more sheltered environment where not-as-hardy life can gain a foothold against the surrounding, more brutal conditions.

Life in Whiplash

Famed for it's long and hot sunny days, it was chillingly windy and actually rained while I was at Joshua Tree National Park. There is a sort of surreal effect of rain, even for a few moments, in the desert. From full sun, to stormy clouds, back to full sun. From completely dry, to soaking wet, to dusty once again. From no leaves, to a full flush of them (like ocotillo) or noticeably expanded (a common trait of cacti). Life is not a matter of constantly sustaining, but of a lurch forward and an abrupt stop to conserve--a different pattern of living that humans are used to.
