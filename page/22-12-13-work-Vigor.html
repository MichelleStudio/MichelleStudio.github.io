Vigor, 2022
digital photography, Nikon D7000.
5400 x 3600 px.
[Joshua Tree National Park]

Close-up detail reveals a variety of life.

Life in the desert takes to a different pace than the one we experience in our human-driven societies. There is no 9 to 5 constant grind of the everyday. Rather, all hinges on a little bit of water. A brief rain revives all, and is followed by a long spell of no change. The dead neither disappears nor is removed, but exists with--even within--the ones that are still alive. Life and time are warped beyond the city normals. In this image, I question our perception of time and the death of all living things when--in reality--it is ever changing.
