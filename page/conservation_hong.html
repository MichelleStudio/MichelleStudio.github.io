Hong Kong: Lay of the Land

REVIEW 2023

Hong Kong is sited at a convergence of extremely dense populations, large swathes of nature reserves, and a deep cultural and emotional attachment to the place itself. A proposal for the future contemporary art gallery of the Asian Art Museum, Hong Kong: Lay of the Land focuses on 1) the effects of hyper-density and the politics of property, 2) nature as a reflection of the Hong Kong experience, and 3) the Hong Kong cultural connection to the physical place itself. Part political intervention, part social commentary, this exhibition questions the identities we invent for ourselves both as individuals and as a community at large.
