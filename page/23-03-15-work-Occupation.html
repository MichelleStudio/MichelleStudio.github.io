Occupation, 2022
wool/nylon blend and polyester, horn toggle buttons.
54”w x 48”h x 2”d
Occupation is a two-piece set, consisting of a long qipao-like coat with a three-tiered cape tied on top. The coat, when worn, takes on a different shape. The underlayer, qipao-like layer is fastened with horn toggle buttons–the somewhat British equivalent of Chinese frog buttons.

Worn, the cloth drapes and looks lively.

When we think of 1800s British imperialism, we think of India or Africa–not so much a small fishing port in China. Nonetheless, after the Opium Wars, Britain as well as France and America, created an "international settlement" in Shanghai–creating an unprecedented pressure to conform to western ideals. Traditional Chinese sewing techniques and philosophies were abandoned. Today, only vague descriptions passed on by word of mouth remain.

In the spirit of camouflage, Occupation takes what remains of Chinese techniques and hides it within a British-looking coat. Cutting on flat, applying jianghu (low-gluten wheat paste), redistributing fabric by steaming, and finishing with traditional bias binding have all been incorporated. Furthermore, the coat is made from more British-like materials. Wool was used instead of a more common cotton/linen/silk as well as horn toggle buttons that mimic the traditional frog buttons. In this way, one might pass as British-style but still remain of Chinese make.
